What do I do if I run out of medication?

Answer

When you come for your appointments, please have your up to date health card and bring all your medications (including over the counter medications and supplements) so that I keep up to date on what you are taking and can renew any prescriptions that are needed at that visit. I do not renew medications over the phone or by fax from your pharmacy as it is time consuming and prone to error. I will give you enough medication until your next regular appointment. On rare occasions, I will phone in enough medication to cover you if your appointment has been changed. Otherwise, it is your responsibility to keep track of your medication. Call the office and you will be fit in for an appointment.

As of 2011, your pharmacist can give you one month's supply of any regular medication you take without calling me first. If you absolutely cannot come in to the office, request 1 month of medication from the pharmacist and call to make an appointment fro refills. The exception to this are the medications below.

As of November 1, 2011, by law, if you need a prescription that is a narcotic (such as codeine or Hycodan syrup), a stimulant (such as Ritalin or Concerta) or a tranquilizer (such as lorazepam or clonazepam) you must show photo ID to get the prescription and I must record that photo ID number on your prescription.

When would I get referred to a Specialist?

Answer

You will be referred to a specialist if a specialized opinion is needed, if a procedure is required or if an operation is necessary. If you prefer a certain specialist or hospital, let me know before the referral is made. I write a referral letter and send a copy of the appropriate tests to the specialist. Finding specialists can be difficult and time consuming. We will contact you ASAP when an appointment has been made; emergency referrals are made immediately. The specialist's office generally determines the waiting time for the appointment; this is out of our hands. Often, we cannot get an appointment time-the specialist's office calls you directly. If your problem gets worse before the appointment with the specialist, please make an appointment with me so that I can reassess you. If you do not hear back about a non urgent appointment within 3 weeks please call my office. Once the referral has been sent, you can call the specialist's office to see if they have a cancellation list for an earlier appointment. Please make sure that you keep the appointment as most specialists bill you for missed appointments and you would need to reschedule that appointment yourself, not through my office. I do not make multiple specialist consultations for the same problem to try to be seen sooner. I generally do not refer for problems that should be followed by a family doctor, for example: routine pap smears, annual breast exams, etc. If you choose to see a specialist without being referred, please make sure that you inform the specialist's office ahead of time that you are doing this outside of OHIP, as I will not provide a referral letter. If you wish to be seen by a dermatologist or plastic surgeon for a cosmetic reason (wrinkles, mole you dislike, skin resurfacing, etc.) this is not covered under OHIP and I do not make a referral. You can find a doctor for this through the Yellow Pages or your personal contacts then call them to make an appointment, realizing that all fees related to this are paid for by you and that all follow up related to that care is to be done there.
